Sugar Rocket

An experiment in creating a solid rocket propellant, known as 'rocket candy', from sugar and potassium nitrate.

The Goal

This project was an introduction to solid-fuel chemistry. The goal was to safely create a stable and effective propellant by carefully mixing and heating sugar and potassium nitrate, and then to test its combustion characteristics in a controlled environment.

My Process & Learnings

  • Carefully measured and combined granulated sugar and potassium nitrate.
  • Heated the mixture slowly and consistently to caramelize the sugar and create a homogenous propellant slurry.
  • Poured the molten propellant into a sturdy cardboard cylinder to form the rocket motor grain.
  • Learned about the critical importance of temperature control and safety precautions when working with chemical propellants.

Gallery

Outcome & Reflection

The propellant was successfully created. For safety reasons, a full flight test was not conducted. Instead, I performed a static fire test by securing the rocket cylinder upside down in a bucket of sand and igniting it. The motor produced a strong, sustained burn, proving the viability of the propellant. This was a valuable lesson in chemical safety and controlled experimentation.
