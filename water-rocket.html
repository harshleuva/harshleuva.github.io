Water & Air Rockets

A classic physics experiment to understand Newton's Third Law by launching a rocket powered by pressurized air and water.

The Goal

The primary goal was to build a simple, reusable rocket and launcher system from everyday materials. I wanted to experimentally determine the optimal air-to-water ratio to achieve the maximum possible altitude, putting Newton's Third Law of Motion into practice.

My Process & Learnings

  • Constructed a stable launchpad using PVC pipes that could securely hold an inverted plastic soda bottle.
  • Attached a standard air pump to a valve on the launchpad to pressurize the bottle.
  • Conducted multiple launches, systematically varying the amount of water in the bottle for each launch (e.g., 1/4 full, 1/3 full, 1/2 full) while keeping the pressure consistent.
  • Visually observed and recorded the approximate apogee (highest point) for each launch to compare the results of different ratios.

Outcome & Reflection

The experiments clearly demonstrated that the rocket's performance is highly dependent on the air/water ratio. I found that a bottle approximately 1/3 full of water provided the best thrust and achieved the greatest height. This project was a fun and effective demonstration of action-reaction principles and the scientific method.
