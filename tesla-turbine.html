Tesla Turbine & Coil

An exploration into Nikola Tesla's bladeless turbine design and high-voltage resonant transformers.

The Goal

I was fascinated by Nikola Tesla's less famous inventions. My goal was to build a functional, bladeless turbine to understand the principles of boundary layer adhesion in fluid dynamics, and to construct a Tesla coil to safely experiment with high-frequency electrical fields.

My Process & Learnings

  • CDs (Compact discs) for the turbine discs were used.
  • Placed the tubine in between a well framed structure for protection.
  • The plate slowed vibration at low speed but at very high speed they become very stable and to ensure that it does not burst a adhesive layer was made on discs.
  • I purposly make the blade exposed so that I was able to see how the air flows in it. For that I used smoke from incense sticks.

Outcome & Reflection

The final turbine successfully spined up to a high RPM using high speed air. The motor was able to light up leds easily. I wish if I could use different materials to make disc and also have made body of turbine.
