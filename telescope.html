Newtonian Reflective Telescope

Building a classic Newtonian telescope from scratch to observe celestial objects.

The Goal

My objective was to build a fully functional reflecting telescope to gain a practical understanding of optics and to be able to observe celestial bodies like the moon and planets with my own handmade instrument.

My Process & Learnings

  • The most challenging part was grinding and polishing the primary mirror to achieve the correct parabolic shape.
  • Constructed the main tube assembly and the mount from simple materials.
  • Learned how to properly align the primary and secondary mirrors (collimation) for a sharp image.
  • This project was a deep dive into both the art and science of telescope making.

Gallery

Outcome & Reflection

The telescope is functional and provides clear views of the moon's craters. The sense of accomplishment from seeing the night sky through an instrument I built myself is immense.
