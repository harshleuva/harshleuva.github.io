Alcohol-Powered Rocket

An experiment in creating a simple combustion-powered rocket using isopropyl alcohol and a custom-built electric spark ignition system.

The Goal

This project was a step up from simple pressure rockets to chemical propulsion. The goal was to build a reliable ignition system from repurposed electronics and to demonstrate a successful launch using the rapid combustion of vaporized isopropyl alcohol.

My Process & Learnings

  • The rocket body was a standard plastic soda bottle, chosen for its ability to handle moderate pressure.
  • I built a simple launchpad from PVC pipe to hold the bottle securely in place before launch.
  • For the ignition, I carefully extracted the high-voltage chipset from an electric mosquito-killing racket.
  • I extended wires from the chipset's output to the launchpad, positioning them inside the pipe to create a spark gap right at the mouth of the bottle.
  • Before launch, a small amount of isopropyl alcohol was sprayed into the bottle and shaken to create a combustible fuel-air vapor.
  • Pressing the racket's button sent a spark to the wires, igniting the vapor and launching the rocket skyward. This was a fantastic lesson in resourcefulness and applied electronics.

Outcome & Reflection

The rocket launched successfully and impressively, proving that a simple, reliable ignition system can be created from common household electronics. The project highlighted the power of rapid gas expansion and was a successful proof-of-concept for basic chemical rocketry.
