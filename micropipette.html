Micropipette

A DIY project to create a functional micropipette, an essential tool in any biology or chemistry lab.

The Goal

Professional lab equipment can be expensive. The goal of this project was to see if a reasonably accurate, functional micropipette could be constructed using simple, accessible materials, putting a fundamental piece of lab gear within reach for home experiments.

My Process & Learnings

  • Designed a plunger system that could displace a specific volume of air to draw in liquid.
  • Used a common syringe as the main body and calibrated the plunger stops for different volumes.
  • The biggest challenge was ensuring an airtight seal to get accurate and repeatable measurements.
  • This project was a great exercise in creative problem-solving and precision mechanics on a small budget.

Gallery

Outcome & Reflection

The final micropipette is capable of dispensing small volumes of liquid with a reasonable degree of accuracy for home experiments. It proves that with a bit of ingenuity, essential scientific tools can be recreated affordably.
